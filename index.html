Peacock Mantis Shrimp

The Peacock Mantis Shrimp has the most complex eyes in the aniamal kingdom, containing millions of light-senstive cells.
With 16 color-receptive cones comapard to humans, who have just three, this crustaceans can dectect ten times more color than a human including ultraviolet.

  • Scientific Name: Odontodactylus Scyllarus
  • Average Length: 2 to 7 inches
  • Average Lifespan: 20 years
  • Habitat: Indian and Pacific Oceans

The Peacock Mantis Shrimp is a territorial creature as it can independently move each eye to avoid it's predators and track down prey.
This ferocious creature has a club like appendage that fold beneath its body, resembling a praying mantis.
With the ablilty to strike at the speed of a .22 caliber bullet, a blow from the mantis shrimp can easily break though the shell of a crab or mollusk.
